Gundam (Japanese: ガンダム Hepburn: Gandamu), also known as the Gundam Series (ガンダムシリーズ Gandamu Shirīzu), is a science fiction media franchise created by Sunrise that features giant robots (mecha) with mobile suits bearing the name Gundam. It began on April 7, 1979 as Mobile Suit Gundam, a TV series that defined the real robot anime genre by featuring giant robots in a militaristic setting. The popularity of the series and its merchandise spawned a franchise that includes television series, OVAs, films, manga, novels, video games and of course, plastic models. (Wikipedia)

Gundam Plastic Models Grades

Gundam Plastic Models or Gunpla have been so popular since the 1980s. And, many have been faithful to the Gundam franchise since then. The first Gunpla ever made was the 1/144 scale 1980 ver. of the RX-78-2 by Bandai. It was release on July 1980. Then also, the 1/60 version of that was made on the same year. Since then, Bandai releases more Gundam kits that are more articulated and stylized models.

High Grade Gundam Kits

On 1990, Bandai release the first High Grade model kits or HG. This grade is usually scaled 1/144. It revolutionized the Gundam model kits at that time where it has better proportions and articulations than the older models of the 1980s. There are also 1/100 and 1/60 scaled Gundam HG kits. This grade has the most production in Gundam model kits because it easey to produce and affordable. The price usually range between $10-$20. This is the recommended kit for beginners.

Master Grade Gundam Kits

On 1995, it releases the first the 1/100 Master Grade models or MG. This grade is always a 1/100 scale kit. This the first kit that has the inner frame. Perfect Grade Gundam Kits

On 1998, It releases the first 1/60 Perfect Grade models or PG. It is the perfect representation of the Gundam model that is why it is called the Perfect Grade. This kit has more parts and more detailed than the MG kits and amazingly detailed inner frame.
